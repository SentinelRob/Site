01 — The Problem

The damage is already happening.

Feral hogs are the most destructive invasive species in the continental United States. They destroy crops, kill livestock, contaminate water sources, carry 30+ diseases including potential FMD vectors, and cause billions in damage annually.

Every existing control method — trapping, aerial gunning, poison baits — fails the same way. None can remove the 66% of the population annually required just to hold numbers flat. The population grows regardless of spending.

This is not a pest management problem. It is a biosecurity crisis operating below the threshold of public awareness.
